Xiuyang Xia

I am a 4th year PhD candidate in the School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences, Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore, and expected to graduate in Dec. 2022. Before entering NTU, Singapore, I got my Bachelor's degree in Materials Sciences in Beijing University of Chemical Technology (BUCT), China.

I am currently working on the soft matter physics, biophysics and statistical mechanics at Computational Soft Matter Lab under the supervision of Prof. Ran Ni and Prof. Massimo Pica Ciamarra. Before that, I worked on the numerical simulation of polymers under the supervision of Prof. Jun Liu and Prof. Liqun Zhang in the Center of Advanced Elastomer Materials in my undergraduate. I also worked in the project of machine learning assisted molecule simulations under the supervision of Prof. Xiaoyu Yang in Computer Network Information Center, Chinese Academy of Sciences as a research intern.

Research

I am interested in the field of soft matter physics and biophysics, ....

Here are lists of research projects I have done and am going to be published.

Entropy driven thermo-gelling vitrimer
Xiuyang Xia#, Peilin Rao#, Juan Yang, Massimo Pica Ciamarra, Ran Ni*
arXiv: 2201.05315 (2022)

We propose a new thermo-gelling vitrimer, which remains at a liquid state at low temerature and forms an equilibrium gel at high temperature.

Entropy-controlled cross-linking in linker-mediated vitrimers
Qun-Li Lei#, Xiuyang Xia#, Juan Yang, Massimo Pica Ciamarra, Ran Ni*
PNAS 117, 27111 (2020)   arXiv / pdf

We formulate a theoretical framework to elucidate the phase behavior of the linker-mediated vitrimers, in which entropy plays a governing role.

Linker-mediated self-assembly of mobile DNA-coated colloids
Xiuyang Xia#, Hao Hu, Massimo Pica Ciamarra*, Ran Ni*
Sci. Adv. 6, eaaz6921 (2020)   arXiv / pdf

The linker-mediated colloidal interaction reaches a plateau at the strong binding limit instead of abrupt change with temperature in conventional systems of DNACCs.

Designing superlattice structure via self-assembly of one-component polymer-grafted nanoparticles
Guanyi Hou#, Xiuyang Xia, Jun Liu*, Wencai Wang*, Mengjie Dong, Liqun Zhang*
J. Phys. Chem. B 123, 2157 (2019)   pdf

Our simulated results provide guidelines to tailor the self-assembly of the one-component polymer-grafted nanoparticles by taking advantage of the length, density, and stiffness of grafted polymer chains.

Theoretical Model of Time–Temperature Superposition Principle of the Self‐Healing Kinetics of Supramolecular Polymer Nanocomposites
Zijian Zheng#, Xiuyang Xia, Xianxiang Zeng, Xiu Li, Youping Wu, Jun Liu*, Liqun Zhang*
Macromol. Rapid Commun. 39, 1800382 (2018)   pdf

A time−temperature superposition principle for the self-healing of the matrix-free polymer nanocomposites is proposed.

Molecular dynamics simulation study of polymer nanocomposites with controllable dispersion of spherical nanoparticles
Zijian Zheng#, Guanyi Hou, Xiuyang Xia, Jun Liu*, Mesfin Tsige*, Youping Wu, Liqun Zhang
J. Phys. Chem. B 121, 43 (2017)   pdf

Our results demonstrate that end-linked polymer nanocomposites combine attractive static and dynamic mechanical properties and exhibit an unusual response to temperature.
